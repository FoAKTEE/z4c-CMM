\section{The characteristic system}
\label{sec:characteristic}

\subsection{Bondi--Sachs framework}
\label{sec:bsframe}

The exterior of the worldtube $r = r_{\rm wt}$ is evolved on outgoing
null slices in Bondi--Sachs form
\cite{Bondi:1962px,Sachs:1962wk,Moxon:2020gha},
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = -\Big(e^{2\beta}\tfrac{V}{r} - r^2 h_{AB}U^AU^B\Big)du^2
 - 2e^{2\beta}\,du\,dr - 2r^2 h_{AB}U^B\,du\,dx^A
 + r^2 h_{AB}\,dx^A dx^B,
\label{eq:bsmetric}
\end{equation}
with the spin-weighted scalars built from the complex dyad $q^A$
($q^Aq_A = 0$, $q^A\bar q_A = 2$): the radiative variable
$J = \tfrac12 q^Aq^Bh_{AB}$, the lapse-like $\beta$, the shift-like
$U = q_AU^A$ with its radial momentum
$Q = r^2 e^{-2\beta} q^A h_{AB}\,\partial_r U^B$, the mass-like
$W = (V-r)/r^2$, and the time derivative $H = \partial_u J$ at fixed
compactified radius \cite{Moxon:2020gha}. The radial coordinate is
compactified as
\begin{equation}
y = 1 - \frac{2\,r_{\rm wt}}{r}\,,
\qquad y \in [-1, 1],\qquad
\frac{dy}{dr} = \frac{(1-y)^2}{2\,r_{\rm wt}}\,,
\label{eq:ycomp}
\end{equation}
so the worldtube sits at $y=-1$ and future null infinity at $y=1$. On
each null slice the Einstein equations order into the radial
hypersurface hierarchy
$\beta \to Q \to U \to W \to H$
(each equation an ODE in $r$ sourced by the previous members and $J$),
followed by the evolution equation $\partial_u J = H$
\cite{Bondi:1962px,Moxon:2020gha,Ma:2023qjn}; we do not reproduce the
nonlinear system here and work instead with its linearization, which the
program verifies term by term.

\subsection{Linear $\ell=2$ scalarization}
\label{sec:scalarization}

\begin{nfblock}
At linear order about Minkowski, the axisymmetric $(\ell,m)=(2,0)$
sector reduces to one real scalar per field [machine-derived and
verified, ledger node N14]: with $s\equiv\sin\theta$,
$c\equiv\cos\theta$, $P\equiv 3c^2-1$,
\begin{equation}
J = J_t\,s^2,\qquad
Q = Q_t\,sc,\qquad
U = U_t\,sc,\qquad
W = W_t\,P,\qquad
\beta = \beta_t\,P,\qquad
H = H_t\,s^2,
\label{eq:tscalars}
\end{equation}
and the $\ell=2$ spin-weighted derivatives close on the same shapes,
\begin{equation}
\eth\beta = -6\,\beta_t\,sc,\qquad
\bar\eth\eth\beta = -6\,\beta_t\,P,\qquad
\eth^2\beta = 6\,\beta_t\,s^2,\qquad
\eth Q = -Q_t\,s^2,\qquad
\eth U = -U_t\,s^2 .
\label{eq:ethclosure}
\end{equation}
The spin-weighted spherical-harmonic modal coefficients used for code
interoperation follow from
\begin{equation}
s^2 = 4\sqrt{\tfrac{2\pi}{15}}\;{}_{2}Y_{20},\qquad
sc = -\sqrt{\tfrac{8\pi}{15}}\;{}_{1}Y_{20},\qquad
P = \sqrt{\tfrac{16\pi}{5}}\;Y_{20}.
\label{eq:modalfactors}
\end{equation}
In the Bondi gauge ($\beta = 0$) the verified linear hierarchy is
\begin{align}
\partial_r\big(r^2 Q_t\big) &= -4\,r^2\,\partial_r J_t,
&
\partial_r U_t &= \frac{Q_t}{r^2},
\nonumber\\
\partial_r\big(r^2 W_t\big) &= 2J_t + 2rU_t + \frac{r^2}{2}\partial_r U_t,
&
\partial_r\big(r H_t\big) &= -\frac{U_t}{2}
 + \frac{r}{4}\,\partial_r^2 J_t - \frac{3J_t}{2r},
\label{eq:plainhierarchy}
\end{align}
where the $H$ row is the plain-family pinned combination; the joint
exact-rational refit on the enlarged (gauge-shifted) solution family
fixes the equivalent covariant form \eqref{eq:beta-hierarchy} below
[derivation artifact \texttt{derive\_beta\_hierarchy.jl}].
\end{nfblock}

\subsection{The $\beta$-corrected hierarchy for live (anchored-gauge) data}
\label{sec:betahierarchy}

\begin{nfblock}
Worldtube data constructed from a live Cauchy state arrive in the
worldtube-anchored gauge of Sec.~\ref{sec:matching}, which carries
$\beta \neq 0$ ($r$-independent at this order) and non-decaying gauge
tails. The hierarchy sources required by such data were machine-derived
as exact-rational fits on the anchored and plain solution families
jointly:
\begin{align}
\partial_r(r^2 Q) &= -\,r^2\,\partial_r(\bar\eth J) - 4r\,\eth\beta,
&
\partial_r U &= Q/r^2,
\nonumber\\
\partial_r(r^2 W) &= \tfrac12\bar\eth\bar\eth J + 2r\,\bar\eth U
 + \tfrac{r^2}{2}\partial_r(\bar\eth U) - \tfrac43\,\bar\eth\eth\beta,
&
\partial_r(rH) &= \tfrac12 r\,\partial_r^2 J + \partial_r J
 + \frac{\eth^2\beta}{r} - \frac{\eth Q}{2r} - \eth U .
\label{eq:beta-hierarchy}
\end{align}
Scalarized with \eqref{eq:tscalars}--\eqref{eq:ethclosure} and recast on
the compactified coordinate \eqref{eq:ycomp}, the system becomes four
ODEs with coefficients regular on the whole interval $y\in[-1,1]$ in
\emph{both} gauges (the $y=1$ rows are automatic regularity conditions):
\begin{align}
2Q_t + (1{-}y)\,Q_t' &= -4(1{-}y)\,J_t' + 24\,\beta_t,
&
U_t' &= \frac{Q_t}{2 r_{\rm wt}},
\nonumber\\
2W_t + (1{-}y)\,W_t' &= \frac{(1{-}y)J_t}{r_{\rm wt}} + 2U_t
 + \frac{(1{-}y)U_t'}{2} + \frac{4\beta_t(1{-}y)}{r_{\rm wt}},
&&
\label{eq:unringed}
\\
H_t + (1{-}y)\,H_t' &=
 \frac{(1{-}y)}{4r_{\rm wt}}\Big[(1{-}y)^2J_t'' - 2(1{-}y)J_t'\Big]
 + \frac{(1{-}y)^2}{2r_{\rm wt}}J_t'
 + \frac{3\beta_t(1{-}y)}{r_{\rm wt}}
 + \frac{Q_t(1{-}y)}{4r_{\rm wt}} + U_t,
&&
\nonumber
\end{align}
with $' = d/dy$. The \emph{ringed} variables $rJ_t$, $r^2U_t$, \dots{}
diverge at $y=1$ on anchored-gauge data (the $k=0,1$ tail monomials);
the unringed form \eqref{eq:unringed} is the representation in which the
anchored solution is finite everywhere [measured failure of the ringed
scheme on anchored data: $\psi_0$ off by $O(10^{3\text{--}4})$; pinned
as a regression test].
\end{nfblock}

\subsection{Spectral solver, $\psi_0$ extraction, and pulse data}
\label{sec:solver}

\begin{nfblock}
The radial direction is discretized on Chebyshev--Gauss--Lobatto nodes
with the standard differentiation matrix \cite{Trefethen:2000}; the linear solves of
\eqref{eq:unringed} use LU factorizations built once, each operator's
boundary row imposing the worldtube value. Time advance is RK4 on
$\partial_u J_t = H_t$ with a simultaneous-approximation-term (SAT) penalty imposition of the
worldtube Dirichlet value \cite{Carpenter:1994},
\begin{equation}
H_t\big|_{y=-1} \mathrel{+}= \tau\,\big(J_t^{\rm wt} - J_t|_{y=-1}\big),
\qquad
\tau = \frac{\sigma N^2}{2\,r_{\rm wt}},\quad \sigma = 1 .
\label{eq:sat}
\end{equation}
The incoming Weyl datum is extracted from $J_t$ alone,
\begin{equation}
\psi_0^t(r) = -\frac{\partial_r J_t}{2r} - \frac{\partial_r^2 J_t}{4},
\qquad
\psi_0 = \psi_0^t\,s^2,
\label{eq:psi0formula}
\end{equation}
an expression \emph{gauge-invariant on the anchored family}: the
anchored tails $a(u) + b(u)/r$ satisfy $\psi_0^t[a + b/r] = 0$
identically, and the machine check
$\psi_0[J_{\rm anchored}] = \psi_0[J_{\rm Bondi}]$ holds as an exact
rational identity [N14]. Evaluation at an interior radius
$r_*\ge r_{\rm wt}$ (required by the matching geometry of
Sec.~\ref{sec:matching}) uses barycentric interpolation at
$y_* = 1 - 2r_{\rm wt}/r_*$ together with
\begin{equation}
\partial_r J_t = \frac{(1{-}y)^2}{2r_{\rm wt}}\,J_t',
\qquad
\partial_r^2 J_t = \frac{(1{-}y)^2}{4r_{\rm wt}^2}
 \Big[(1{-}y)^2 J_t'' - 2(1{-}y)J_t'\Big];
\label{eq:proberules}
\end{equation}
the interior point is \emph{better} conditioned than the worldtube node
(measured: evolved-probe relative error $8.8\times10^{-5}$ vs the
$\sim\!6\times10^{-2}$ worldtube floor at the same resolution and step).
For the pulse-injection test of Sec.~\ref{sec:tests} the initial null
slice carries the compactly supported $\ell=2$ free datum specified in
the test design of Ref.~\cite{Ma:2023qjn} (polynomial envelope times
Gaussian in $y$, amplitude $Z$, support $[y_{\min},y_{\max}]$, center
$y_c$, width $\tau_y$), converted to the $t$-scalar convention by
\begin{equation}
J_t(y)\big|_{u_0} = \frac14\sqrt{\frac{15}{2\pi}}\;\mathcal J(y),
\qquad
\mathcal J(y) = \frac{4Z\,(y_{\max}-y)(y-y_{\min})}
{(y_{\max}-y_{\min})^2}\,e^{-(y-y_c)^2/\tau_y^2}
\;\;\text{on } [y_{\min},y_{\max}],
\label{eq:pulseid}
\end{equation}
and zero outside the support.
\end{nfblock}

\subsection{Exact Teukolsky reference in Bondi variables}
\label{sec:teukbondi}

\begin{nfblock}
The solver verifier is the exact linear Teukolsky solution
(Sec.~\ref{sec:teuktest}, Eqs.~\eqref{eq:teuk-F}--\eqref{eq:teuk-metric})
transformed to the Bondi gauge; the machine-derived closed forms are
\begin{align}
J &= \tfrac34\,s^2\Big[\frac{F^{(4)}}{r} + \frac{F^{(2)}}{r^3}\Big],
\qquad
\beta = 0,
\qquad
H = \partial_u J,
\nonumber\\
Q &= sc\Big[\frac{3F^{(4)}}{r} - \frac{9F^{(3)}}{r^2}
 - \frac{9F^{(2)}}{r^3}\Big],
\qquad
U = sc\Big[-\frac{3F^{(4)}}{2r^2} + \frac{3F^{(3)}}{r^3}
 + \frac{9F^{(2)}}{4r^4}\Big],
\nonumber\\
W &= P\Big[-\frac{3F^{(4)}}{2r^2} - \frac{3F^{(3)}}{2r^3}
 - \frac{3F^{(2)}}{4r^4}\Big],
\label{eq:teukbondi}
\end{align}
all arguments retarded, $F^{(n)} = F^{(n)}(u)$ with $u = t - r$. The
hierarchy \eqref{eq:plainhierarchy} closes on \eqref{eq:teukbondi} as a
rational identity; the incoming datum is
\begin{equation}
\psi_0 = -\tfrac98\,s^2\,\frac{F^{(2)}(u)}{r^5}
\qquad\text{(exactly; no subleading retarded terms)},
\label{eq:teukpsi0}
\end{equation}
consistent with \eqref{eq:teuk-psi0}, and the outgoing waveform is the
finite-radius expression \eqref{eq:teuk-exact-psi4} with the convention
map stated there. An independent production characteristic code run on
worldtube data built from \eqref{eq:teukbondi} reproduces
$\Psi_4 = -\sqrt{6\pi/5}\,F^{(6)}$ at future null infinity to
$6\times10^{-6}$ relative and \eqref{eq:teukpsi0} to $0.1\%$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:tests}).
\end{nfblock}

\begin{nfblock}
\subsection{Solver verification summary}
\label{sec:liveccm}

(i) Exact-slice sweeps of \eqref{eq:unringed} are machine-exact
($\sim\!10^{-22}$) on both the Bondi and anchored families. (ii) The
SAT evolution reproduces the worldtube $\psi_0$ at the documented
double-precision conditioning floor and the interior probe
\eqref{eq:proberules} at $8.8\times10^{-5}$ relative
($7.1\times10^{-6}$ at halved step; truncation-dominated), identically
for plain and anchored boundary data. (iii) The native C++ production
port is gated against the verification-harness numbers (standalone
test; relative agreement $\sim\!10^{-7}$). (iv) The pulse problem
\eqref{eq:pulseid} is radially converged at $n=65$ collocation points
to $2.1\times10^{-7}$ of peak (resolution ladder), with time-stepping
error $4\times10^{-12}$. (v) The constraint-row boundary analysis and
the live two-way coupling are treated in Secs.~\ref{sec:cauchy} and
\ref{sec:matching}--\ref{sec:tests} respectively.
\end{nfblock}
